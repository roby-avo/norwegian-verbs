About irregular verbs (Om uregelrette verb)

English: In Norwegian, irregular verbs are grouped as weak (svake) or strong (sterke). Weak verbs take an ending in the past tense, often -et, -te or -de. Strong verbs do not take a past‑tense ending and instead change their vowel. These patterns help you predict the verb forms.

Norsk: På norsk deles uregelrette verb i svake og sterke. Svake verb får en endelse i preteritum, for eksempel -et, -te eller -de. Sterke verb får ingen endelse, men skifter vokal. Disse mønstrene hjelper deg å forutsi verbformene.

Some verbs have several alternative forms. Those verbs are classified as hard and will require more practice. You can choose easy, medium or hard mode using the difficulty selector.
